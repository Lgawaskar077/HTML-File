Pav Bhaji Recipe

Pav Bhaji is a popular street-style Indian dish that combines a spicy, thick vegetable curry (bhaji) with buttered bread rolls (pav). The bhaji is made from boiled and mashed potatoes, cauliflower, and green peas, cooked with finely chopped onions, tomatoes, and capsicum in a mixture of butter and oil. The dish gets its distinctive flavor from pav bhaji masala, red chilli powder, turmeric, and ginger-garlic paste. The vegetables are thoroughly mashed to create a smooth, thick consistency and simmered for 8-10 minutes. The finishing touches include lemon juice, fresh coriander, and a generous knob of butter on top. The pav is prepared by slitting the bread rolls horizontally and toasting them on a hot tawa with butter until they achieve a golden, crisp exterior. This dish is served hot with the bhaji topped with extra butter, chopped raw onions, and a lemon wedge on the side, making it perfect for dinner or weekend gatherings.

Pav Preparation

Slit pav horizontally.
Toast on a hot tawa with butter until golden and crisp.

Serving

Serve hot bhaji topped with butter, chopped onion, lemon wedge, and toasted pav on the side. This version is street-style, rich, and full of flavor—perfect for dinner or a weekend treat.F

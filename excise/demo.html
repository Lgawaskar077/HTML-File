Pav bhaji

Ingredients

  • 3 medium potatoes (boiled & mashed)
  • 1 cup cauliflower (finely chopped, boiled)
  • ½ cup green peas (boiled)
  • 1 large onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 large tomato (finely chopped)
  • 1 green capsicum (finely chopped)
  • 2 tbsp butter (plus extra for serving)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 2 tsp pav bhaji masala
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder (adjust to taste)
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • Fresh coriander (chopped)
  • 6–8 pav (bread rolls)

Methods


Heat butter and oil together in a pan. Add chopped onions and sauté until light golden.
Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute until raw smell disappears.
Add capsicum and sauté for 2–3 minutes until slightly soft.
Add tomatoes, turmeric, chilli powder, pav bhaji masala, and salt. Cook until the mixture turns soft
and oil starts separating.
Add boiled potatoes, cauliflower, and peas. Mash everything well using a potato masher.
Add a little water to get a thick but smooth consistency. Simmer for 8–10 minutes, stirring often.
Finish with lemon juice and fresh coriander. Add a knob of butter on top.

Pav Preparation

Slit pav horizontally.
Toast on a hot tawa with butter until golden and crisp.

Serving

Serve hot bhaji topped with butter, chopped onion, lemon wedge, and toasted pav on the side. This version is street-style, rich, and full of flavor—perfect for dinner or a weekend treat.F

Masala Dosa

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dosa rice
  • ½ cup urad dal
  • ¼ tsp fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste
  • Oil or ghee for cooking dosa
  • 3 medium potatoes (boiled & mashed)
  • 1 large onion (thinly sliced)
  • 2 green chillies (finely chopped)
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 8–10 curry leaves
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • Fresh coriander (chopped)

Methods


Wash and soak rice, urad dal, and fenugreek seeds for 6–8 hours.
Grind to a smooth batter, add salt, and ferment overnight.
Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them crackle.
Add cumin seeds, curry leaves, green chillies, and onions. Sauté until soft.
Add turmeric powder and mashed potatoes. Mix well and cook for 2–3 minutes.
Add chopped coriander and keep aside.
Heat a tawa and pour a ladle of dosa batter. Spread thin in circular motion.
Drizzle oil around edges and cook until golden and crisp.
Place potato masala in the center and fold the dosa.

Dosa Preparation

Use medium heat for crisp dosa.
Spread batter evenly and avoid thick layers.

Serving

Serve hot masala dosa with coconut chutney and sambar. Perfect for breakfast or light dinner.

Punjabi sabji

Paneer Butter Masala

Ingredients

  • 200 g paneer (cubed)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (pureed)
  • 1 medium onion (pureed)
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 2 tbsp fresh cream
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander (chopped)

Methods


Heat oil and butter in a pan.
Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté lightly.
Add onion puree and cook till golden.
Add tomato puree, spices, and salt. Cook until oil separates.
Add paneer cubes and mix gently.
Add cream and simmer for 5 minutes.
Garnish with fresh coriander.

Tips

Use fresh paneer for best taste.

Serving

Serve hot with naan, roti, or jeera rice.
Author : Ankit Patel

Punjabi Chole Masala

Ingredients

  • 1 cup chickpeas (soaked overnight & boiled)
  • 2 medium onions (finely chopped)
  • 2 medium tomatoes (pureed)
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1½ tsp chole masala
  • 1 tsp red chilli powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander (chopped)

Methods


Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds.
Add onions and sauté until golden brown.
Add ginger-garlic paste and cook briefly.
Add tomato puree, spices, and salt. Cook well.
Add boiled chickpeas and mix.
Add little water and simmer for 10 minutes.
Garnish with fresh coriander.

Tips

Slow cooking enhances flavor.

Serving

Serve hot with bhature, kulcha, or steamed rice.
Author : Ankit Patel
